The authors discuss the assessment and treatment of anorexia nervosa (AN) and consider how AN can he most effectively treated in a managed care environment. They describe the diagnostic criteria for AN and the vicious cycle associated with this devastating disorder, as well as personality features, comorbid mental disorders, and medical complications that are frequently associated with AN. They describe a comprehensive assessment of AN, including how to deal with denial and the rationale for psychological testing in this disorder. The authors discuss how to choose the most appropriate treatment setting for a particular patient at a specific point in treatment and review the latest medical, psychopharmacological, psychoeducational, and psychotherapeutic treatments for AN.
