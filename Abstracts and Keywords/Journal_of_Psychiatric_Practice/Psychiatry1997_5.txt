The authors discuss the importance of early identification and treatment of postpartum disorders. They then review the assessment, clinical characteristics, and psychotherapeutic and psychopharmacologic treatment of four major types of postpartum psychiatric conditions: postpartum blues, postpartum depression, postpartum psychosis, and postpartum anxiety disorders. The authors discuss the possible role of biologic factors (e.g., hormonal shifts) in the etiology of postpartum disorders. The article concludes with a detailed discussion of the use of psychotropic medications in breastfeeding mothers.
