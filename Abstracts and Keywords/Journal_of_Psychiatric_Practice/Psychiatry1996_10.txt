The author, who has worked in the field of health information management for 21 years, begins with a brief overview of the history of reimbursement coding. She then provides practical guidelines to help clinicians avoid pitfalls in documentation and improve the accuracy and quality of their record keeping. The author, who specializes in mental health/substance abuse issues, acted as a consultant for the American Psychiatric Association in the development of the DSM-IV coding and classification and is the author of the DSM-N to ICD-9-CM Cross-walk published by the American Health Information Management Association. She has lectured and conducted workshops on topics such as confidentiality and legal issues, documentation and information systems, and mental health/substance abuse coding.
