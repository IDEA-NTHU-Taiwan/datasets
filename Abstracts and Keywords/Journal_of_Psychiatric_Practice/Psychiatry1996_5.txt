In the following article, the authors review the current state of our knowledge of sleep abnormalities associated with mental and addictive disorders. They first discuss scientific advances in understanding sleep in chronobiology, sleep physiology, and genetics. The bulk of the article describes specific types of sleep disruptions associated with psychoses, mood disorders, anxiety disorders, eating disorders, somatoform disorders, adjustment disorders, dissociative disorders, and addictive disorders. The article concludes with a discussion of sleep issues related to life cycle and gender. The extensive and well-documented information on the role of sleep disorders in mental and addictive disorders should help clinicians diagnose, treat, and even prevent comorbid sleep and mental disorders.
