The authors review the latest information on the diagnosis and treatment of social phobia. They discuss how to recognize generalised and performance subtypes of social phobia, how to differentiate normal shyness from social phobia, and describe the most common comorbid disorders. They then review the cognitive -behavioral treatment of social phobia and give a detailed description of cognitive-behavioral group treatment (CBGT). The article concludes with a detailed discussion of pharma-cotherapy for social phobia, including how to select a medication, appropriate dosing patterns, how to manage side effects that may be particularly troublesome for patients with soical phobia, and how and when to combine cognitive-behavioural therapy and medication.
